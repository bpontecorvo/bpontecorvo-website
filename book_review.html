A complete, thorough history of our beloved Killer Whales, from the capture-era to present day news, a must read for any orca or PNW history fanatic. It is interesting from a biology perspective, as well as just a human policy/interaction viewpoint. The Southern Resident Killer Whales are the most well studied group of Cetaceans on the planet- we know who everyone's parent, siblings, etc are, what they eat, who is born, who dies.. Thoughtfully written and well researched, this book contains everything the lay person would/could want to know. While it is possible that they make a comeback, it is unlikely unless they start finding more Chinook Salmon very soon, or else the calves will keep dying.
