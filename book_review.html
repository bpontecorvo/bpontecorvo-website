The Autobiography of Gucci Man - Radric Davis, aka Gucci, can tell a story as well as he can write a hit song. From poverty, violence, prison, drug addiction, to inventing a new genre of music Gucci has lived a crazy life. Love him or hate him, Gucci gives a raw, honest retelling of his life and the crazy shit that happened along the way.
ğŸ¤ ğŸ¤ ğŸ¤ ğŸ¤

I received this as a birthday gift from a friend. This book is never dull and reasonably well written; the author retells stories coherently but still keeps his voice. It was fantastic to learn about his process for creating music, working with Zaytoven to make beats, and where he got his musical ideas from. There were countless times throughout his life that Gucci quickly could have died; it is frankly shocking that he made it out alive. It also made me wonder how many talented and young minds are killed only because they are products of the environments they grew up in. I would recommend this to anyone interested in Musicians, Trap Music, ATL, or learning more about the Black experience in the US.

Written by a leading climate expert, this is a comprehensive collection of PNW storm records, weather processes, meteorological phenomena, and predictions about our climate in the next 50-100 years. This book is part meteorology 101, part PNW storm history, and jam-packed with exciting graphics and charts.

It is digestible by both the expert and novice weather enthusiast, and you can skip over dull sections. Chapters cover everything from the basics, to floods, storms, coastal weather features, mountain-related phenomena, to snowpack, forest fires, to climate change and weather prediction challenges. The author walks you through how meteorologists make forecasts, both historically and to this day. I learned that the weather in the PNW is the MOST PREDICTABLE in the nation. Some parts are quite dry, but you can just skip them, and some graphics are shockingly low quality and hard to understand. Unlike his blog, you will find no politics here.
